There is **no universal national “cooling-off period”** that lets you casually unwind a signed mortgage renewal the way some consumer purchases work. Search interest is high; the legal reality is narrower.

## What people usually mean

- Changing your mind after signing a **renewal agreement** with your current lender
- Backing out of a **new lender commitment** after conditions are waived
- Confusing renewal with a **purchase agreement** rescission right (provincial and situation-specific)

## Practical rule

Treat a signed renewal or waived commitment as **binding**. Shop rates and read privileges **before** you sign. If something material changes (rate not as disclosed, conditions unmet), talk to your broker and lawyer immediately — do not assume a free multi-day undo window.

## Better than a cooling-off period

Use the **120-day** shopping window. Compare written offers. Only sign the winner.
