If you ignore your renewal letter, many Canadian lenders will still **roll you onto a new term** — often at a rate that is easy for them and expensive for you.

## What “automatic” usually means

Federally regulated lenders must send renewal information ahead of maturity (commonly inside a **21–120 day** window). If you do not respond, the lender may renew you onto a default product. That product is frequently closer to **posted** or lightly discounted pricing than the broker-channel rates available the same week.

## Why inertia is expensive

July 2026 broker-channel insured 5-year fixed renewals sit near **~4.04%** (variable near **~3.35%**). Retention letters commonly clear **0.25%–0.75%** higher. On a $500,000 balance, half a percent is thousands of dollars over a five-year term.

## What to do instead

1. **120 days out** — request competing quotes and rate holds.
2. Decode the letter — term, rate type, prepayment privileges, charge type.
3. Compare **stay vs switch** including discharge and legal costs.
4. Sign only after the math wins — never because the deadline email sounded urgent.
