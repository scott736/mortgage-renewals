Yes — **maturity is the designed exit**. When the term ends, you can pay the balance in full without an interest-rate differential (IRD) or three-month interest break penalty that would apply mid-term.

## What you may still pay

- **Discharge / assignment fee** on the outgoing mortgage (lender-specific; often a few hundred dollars)
- **Legal / notary** fees to clear title if you are selling or moving debt elsewhere
- Any **accrued interest** to the payout date

## Partial paydown at renewal

Many borrowers use annual **prepayment privileges** just before maturity, then renew a smaller balance. Check your commitment for lump-sum and payment-increase limits so you do not accidentally trigger a penalty by overpaying mid-term.

## Breaking early is different

Paying out before maturity is a **break**, not a renewal payout — IRD or three-month interest usually applies. Run numbers before you chase a slightly better rate mid-term.
